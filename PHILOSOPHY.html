My Philosophy

I personally believe that everyone should have access to a education as well as have the right to choose what they want to do. The last thing i want for someone is to force them in a area of study that they hate. I want to teach people that want to be taught in the area of technology so that they can use me as a stepping stone to further them selves to move further. I know when i enter this career, there will be students that will not like technology such as woodwork or metal work for it is either too hard or not for them but, I will still teach indiscriminately the subject to the best of my ability to ensure that all students are able to specialise in the taught area or have at least taken something away.

when I talk about my personal beliefs, it can be separated into categories:

- Truth academic

- excellence equity

- Accessibility

- Wellbeing

the reason why these are the categories in which i line up my personal belief with is because, Truth should be the core of both the student and the teacher for if we were to be untruthful in what we communicate to others, problems will arise and won't be able to be resolved Academic excellence for, I want all my students try their best every step of the way as well as be academically just in the sense that it is their work. if students are not going to try in any subject and copy other's work, how are you expected to learn. I am heavy on fairness. ensuring that Equity is available 100% of the time is the bare minimum when it comes to teaching. understanding my learners and how to teach them plays a huge role when being fair for not all students are equal meaning that students will of course needs different requirements to further them self. Providing accessibility to education as a whole as well as the accessibility to resources such as materials and other elements of the unit is necessary to teach as well as help engage students in the classroom. Wellbeing, making sure that students feel safe and welcomed into any class room enviroment is I belive a nessesity in conjuntion with the every other point present.
