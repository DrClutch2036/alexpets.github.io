Welcome to Alexander's Portfolio

My name is Alexander Petsoglou, a 21-year-old student, aiming to become one of the best Industrial Technology teachers the teaching industry has seen. it has been hard to find a place that I would belong to for my hobbies and interests are unique and specific. you are probably wondering how this all started. it was year 11 and I being me was wondering what I should do after I have finished high school. I have always enjoyed creating and making items such as furniture to technology and am well versed in both regards. I have always loved the technology area and what it has to offer. I have always loved to design either items of furniture or design devices to either automate tedious tasks or to assist with other common problems.

Many people have influenced who I am today, pushing me to be better so that when my knowledge is needed, it can be used to help someone else. I hear from people from high school and university saying things like "Why do we need to learn this" or "This isn't going to help me" or "This topic is boring" I ask myself why. I have noticed that people are only in this for themselves without thinking about those they will help. I collect and gather as much information regardless of if it is in my area of teaching or not for it might come in handy someday that might help a student or a fellow teacher. teachers are forever learners and academics Technology as a whole has always been something I have held dear to me. it was an interest that I have had for so long that wanted to invest my life towards the progress as well as the education of technology to further better the environment around me. I follow the motto that "repeating the same thing expecting different results is called insanity". I want to be different to the other teachers for I already know how effective their work is. i want to try new things with new technologies to both accelerate and better reinforce these concepts and ideas so that the students can create and further themselves to far exceed our generation to greater heights. it is unfortunate that I won't be able to see the future I am helping build, however, legacy is leaving seeds in a garden you never get to see.
